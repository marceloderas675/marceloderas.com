About Me

Hi, I’m Marcelo Deras. I’m currently studying Electrical and Computer Engineering at Cornell University, where I’m actively involved in the Cornell Maker Club as a Trainer on the Executive Board.

Whether I’m leading safety trainings in the lab, assisting others with equipment like 3D printers and soldering irons, or developing projects from scratch, I'm always thinkering and learning.

Before Cornell, I led the design efforts for my FTC Robotics Team 21503, where I helped bring home a Connecticut State Record and a World Tournament Qualification.

My interest in robotics has carried over into college, where I’ve continued building projects like a self-balancing robot and a custom electrocardiogram (ECG) device using filters and Arduino.

Outside of engineering, I’ve worked with the Ithaca ReUse Center, helping intake and process hundreds of donated items sustainably.

I'm especially interested in embedded systems, analog circuits, and 3D modeling. I'm excited to learn through more projects!
